The Gregorian Calendar

The US uses the Gregorian calendar, introduced by Pope Gregory XIII on October 15, 1582. Of the 2026 years since year 0, 1582 passed before it existed. It has only been in use for approximately 444 years — roughly 21.9% of that span.

Before Gregorian, the dominant system was the Julian calendar (Julius Caesar, 46 BC). The Gregorian fix corrected an 11-minute annual drift that had accumulated to a 10-day error. When adopted, 10 days were skipped overnight. Britain and its American colonies didn't adopt it until 1752. Some countries waited until the 20th century — Russia in 1918, Greece in 1923.
